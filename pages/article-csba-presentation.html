Overview

This presentation introduces H-EDU's approach to accountability and verification for California school boards. It covers the achievement gap crisis, the role of data in driving improvement, and how VERA (Verification Engine for Results & Accountability) enables boards to identify students who are falling through the cracks.

Key Topics

  • The Achievement Gap — California spends $146 billion annually on education, yet fewer than half of students read at grade level
  • The Type 4 Gap — Students who speak well but write poorly, often misclassified and receiving inappropriate interventions
  • VERA Technology — MCP-native verification engine connecting AI to California assessment data (CAASPP, ELPAC)
  • Board-Level Accountability — How school boards can use data verification to drive LCAP compliance and student outcomes
