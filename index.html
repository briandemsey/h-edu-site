PROOF OF CONCEPT EXISTS

Programs that actually closed the achievement gap.

Mississippi

Science of Reading + 3rd-Grade Gate + Accountability

49th to Top 10 in 4th-grade reading. Black students #3 nationally; Hispanic students #1.

Cost: $32/student

Tennessee

Reading 360 + Math Support Act + Curriculum Reform

4th-grade math ranking jumped 10 places in one year. 8th-grade reading growth #1 nationally.

Gains across all grades

Louisiana

READ Louisiana + Back-to-Basics Accountability

Only state to surpass pre-pandemic NAEP levels. 4th-grade reading: 15th nationally — highest-ever ranking.

Post-pandemic recovery leader

Harlem Children's Zone

Cradle-to-Career Wraparound Model

Closed the Black-white math gap entirely. 99% of 2025 seniors accepted to college.

Replicated in 600+ organizations globally

AI: Tutor CoPilot

Stanford RCT — AI coaching for tutors, not students

4-9 percentage point math mastery gains. Largest gains for weakest tutors (9 pp). Levels the playing field at scale.

Cost: ~$20/tutor/year

THE RECORD

What failed — and why. $190 billion in ESSER funds with no verified outcomes.

Whole Language / Three-Cueing

Taught children to guess words from context instead of decode. Decades of use despite contrary science.

Ideology over evidence

NCLB-Era Tutoring

Poorly trained tutors, embezzlement scandals, contracts paid for 1,100 students when 200 showed up.

No verification

Federal School Turnarounds

Mass firings of teachers and principals, school closures. Disruption produced no measurable gains.

Wrong theory of change

Vouchers & Private School Lotteries

Multiple rigorous studies found no consistent academic gains. Moving students doesn't transfer quality.

Misdiagnosed problem

Oklahoma's Defanged Retention Law

Copied Mississippi's law in 2012, then repealed accountability teeth in 2014. Scores plummeted.

Political retreat

Post-ESSER Spending ($190B)

Record spending on tutoring, technology, and staffing. Most districts cannot produce credible evidence of outcomes.

Inputs without outcomes
"The failure mode is the same every time: spend without verifying, reform without measuring, retreat when accountability becomes uncomfortable."
